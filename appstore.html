About the iNethi App Store

The iNethi App Store is an innovative platform designed to enhance digital inclusion for underserved communities by providing localized apps and services. Developed for the Ocean View Community Wireless Network (CWN) in South Africa, the iNethi App Store empowers residents to access essential digital content, such as community radio and educational tools, even without active internet connectivity.

This app store aims to tackle one of the most pressing challenges faced by low-income communities — the high cost of mobile internet and limited access to digital resources. By consolidating local services into a single, user-friendly interface, the iNethi App Store not only promotes local engagement but also fosters community development. With features like offline functionality and pre-installed apps, the platform ensures that critical digital services are accessible to everyone, irrespective of their connectivity status.

Through a participatory design approach, the iNethi App Store was co-created with the community, making it a platform built for and by its users. Join us in exploring how technology can transform communities and bridge the digital divide.

Context

The iNethi App Store project is focused on addressing digital access challenges in Ocean View, South Africa, a community with limited internet connectivity and high data costs. Partnering with Black Equations, a local Wireless Internet Service Provider (WISP), the project leverages the iNethi Community Wireless Network to offer zero-rated, locally-hosted services. This collaboration ensures that residents can access essential digital resources without incurring high costs.

The app store is built to provide a gateway to community-driven content, empowering residents to explore and download apps tailored to their needs, such as educational tools and local media. This approach is informed by similar initiatives like DakNet, which highlight the value of local networks in underserved regions but often lack sustained engagement. By focusing on usability, offline functionality, and local participation, the iNethi App Store aims to overcome these limitations and promote long-term digital inclusion.

Design Process

The iNethi App Store was developed using a participatory design approach, which involved continuous collaboration with the Ocean View community. This methodology ensured that the app addressed the specific needs and preferences of its users. The development process was structured around iterative cycles of planning, implementation, and evaluation, guided by three main techniques: Participatory Design (PD), Action Research (AR), and Cognitive Walkthroughs (CW).

Participatory Design (PD)

Community members were engaged as active participants in co-design workshops, providing input on the app's features and usability. These workshops allowed users to express their needs, test various app prototypes, and suggest changes, ensuring that the final product was highly relevant and user-friendly.

Action Research (AR)

The development team adopted AR to adapt to the evolving requirements of the community. Each development cycle involved planning, acting, observing, and reflecting, based on user feedback collected during workshops. This flexible approach enabled the project to remain responsive to the dynamic community environment.

Cognitive Walkthroughs (CW)

Usability testing was conducted through cognitive walkthroughs, where participants were guided through different app functions to identify potential usability issues. This method helped simulate the experience of first-time users and informed improvements to the app’s interface.

Results

  • Usability feedback from the community identified missing features such as search functionality and app ratings, which limited user experience and engagement.
  • Adoption challenges were observed due to the sporadic availability of the iNethi network and the incomplete setup of the F-Droid server.
  • Participatory design facilitated effective co-design, enabling users to influence the app’s final interface and feature set.
  • High interest in offline content highlighted the value of preinstalled apps, which users found beneficial in low-bandwidth scenarios.
  • Successful integration of monitoring tools like Amplitude and Grafana allowed detailed tracking of app usage and real-time feedback.
  • Positive reception of the offline-first approach was noted, as it reduced dependency on the network and supported local access to key apps.
